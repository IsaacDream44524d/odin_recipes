Cranberry Sauce

This Cranberry Sauce recipe combines fresh cranberries, sugar and orange juice for a quick and easy side that adds a burst of tangy flavor to your Thanksgiving holiday meal.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces of Fresh Cranberry: Opt for fresh cranberries that are firm, plump, and blemish-free. Lighter colored cranberries are best for sauce, as they contain more pectin and result in a thicker consistency.
  • 1 cup of White Sugar: White sugar does more than add sweetness to classic cranberry sauce: It also absorbs liquid and, when heated, thickens the sauce.
  • 1 cup of Orange Juice All cranberry sauce starts with a liquid base. If you don't like orange juice, you can substitute pomegranate juice or even red wine.

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Dissolve sugar in orange juice in a medium saucepan over medium heat.Overhead shot of a pan with dissolving sugar in orange juice next to fresh cranberries
  3. Stir in cranberries and cook until they start to pop and sauce starts to thicken, about 8 to 10 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and place sauce in a bowl. The sauce will continue to thicken as it cools.
  5. Enjoy
